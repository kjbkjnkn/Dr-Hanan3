Module 09 • Risk Management

Brand Crisis & Reputation

Session Focus

Protecting brand equity and trust by responding strategically and authentically in an environment where controversy scales in minutes.

1. The Speed of Digital Crises

In the digital age, silence is interpreted as guilt. Crises are amplified by "screenshot culture" and algorithmic controversy loops.

The Golden Hour

The first 60 minutes define the narrative. Brands must acknowledge the issue and set expectations even before full details are known.

2. Recovery Strategies

Phase A: Response

Show empathy, take responsibility, and avoid defensive "corporate speak."

Phase B: Corrective

Explain what went wrong and how policies will change internally.

Phase C: Rebuild

Demonstrate learning through visible, long-term consistent actions.

3. "Cancel Culture" & Accountability

Modern audiences demand value alignment and ethical behavior. Brands are now treated as public figures with social responsibilities.

Platform-Specific Nuance:
  • Twitter/X: Conciseness and speed.
  • Instagram: Human-centric, visual transparency (Stories).

Activity: The Crisis Statement (15 mins)

Scenario: A beverage brand accidentally posts an offensive meme.

Task A: Twitter Apology
Focus: Stop escalation via speed and clear accountability.
Task B: IG Story Explanation
Focus: Human tone and value-alignment reassurance.

Key Takeaways

  • Digital crises are inevitable; poor responses are not.
  • Speed, empathy, and responsibility define success.
  • Trust is rebuilt through visible action, not just statements.
