Module 04 • Visual Strategy

Brand Identity & Visual Systems

Session Focus

Transforming brand strategy into visual and sensory assets that work seamlessly across digital platforms, ensuring flexibility and psychological resonance.

1. Core Visual Elements

Modern visual systems must prioritize accessibility and screen readability over static aesthetics.

Logo Systems

Not just one logo, but a responsive set.

  • App Avatars
  • Social Favicons
  • Dark Mode Adapts

Color Psychology

Influencing trust and perceived quality.

  • Blue: Trust & Stability
  • Red: Energy & Urgency
  • Green: Growth & Health

Typography

Communicating brand personality.

  • Serif: Traditional
  • Sans: Modern/Clean
  • Readability is Key

2. Digital Elasticity

Digital Elasticity refers to a brand’s ability to adapt to different platforms and scale without losing its core identity.

Strategic Evolution: Guidelines Shift

Move from Static Consistency (Print) to Dynamic Coherence (Digital). Guidelines must now include motion rules, micro-interactions, and dark/light mode versions.

Activity: The Mood Board (20 mins)

Brief: Create a mini mood board for a personal brand using Canva or Pinterest.

Constraints & Requirements:

  • Maximum 3 colors and 2 fonts.
  • Explain the psychological reasoning behind choices.
  • Demonstrate how the visuals reflect your personal positioning.

Key Takeaways

  • Brand architecture shapes digital clarity and scalability.
  • Visual identity is strategic communication, not decoration.
  • Digital-first branding requires flexibility and psychological insight.
