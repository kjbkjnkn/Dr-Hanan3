From IMC to Brand Management

Session Objective

By the end of this session, students should be able to:

  • Clearly distinguish a product from a brand
  • Understand Brand Equity and why it is a strategic asset
  • Explain Keller's Brand Equity Pyramid
  • Analyze how digital touchpoints shape brand perceptions
  • Apply concepts through a Brand Audit of digital-native brands

Discussion Starter

Question to Consider:

"If Uber and another ride-hailing app offer the same price, same car, same route‚Äîwhy would someone still prefer Uber?"

Use this to transition:

  • IMC (Integrated Marketing Communications) focuses on consistent messaging
  • Brand Management goes deeper: It focuses on how people feel, remember, and trust a company over time
Key idea: IMC builds messages. Brand Management builds meaning.

Product vs. Brand

Product

A product is:

  • Tangible or functional
  • Defined by features, price, quality, and usage
  • Easily copied by competitors

Examples:

  • A ride from Point A to Point B
  • A mobile payment transfer
  • A food delivery service

Brand

A brand is:

  • A set of associations, emotions, and expectations
  • What people say about you when you're not in the room
  • A shortcut for decision-making

Includes:

  • Trust
  • Reputation
  • Personality
  • Social meaning
  • Emotional reassurance
Products are made in factories. Brands are built in minds.

Key Difference

  • Products answer: "What does it do?"
  • Brands answer: "Why should I choose you?"

Understanding Brand Equity

What Is Brand Equity?

Brand Equity is the extra value a brand gives to a product beyond its functional benefits.

In simple terms:
Brand Equity = Why people are willing to pay more, wait longer, or forgive mistakes

Examples to Discuss

  • Why do people forgive Uber more easily than a new ride-hailing app?
  • Why does Apple survive product mistakes better than lesser-known brands?
  • Why would someone trust InstaPay with money transfers faster than a new app?

Keller's Brand Equity Pyramid

Keller's model represents a step-by-step relationship between brand and customer, building from awareness to deep loyalty.

Level 1: Brand Salience ‚Äì Who are you?

Focus: Awareness and recall

  • How easily the brand comes to mind
  • Top-of-mind recall

Digital Examples:

  • App store rankings
  • Search results visibility
  • Social media presence
  • Push notifications

üí° Example: Uber is often the first app people think of when they need a ride.

Level 2: Brand Performance & Brand Imagery ‚Äì What are you?

Brand Performance (Functional):

  • Reliability
  • Speed
  • Ease of use
  • Security

Brand Imagery (Emotional & Social):

  • Modern vs traditional
  • Premium vs affordable
  • Local vs global
  • "For people like me"

Digital Touchpoints Involved:

  • App UX/UI design
  • Loading speed
  • Error messages tone
  • Notification style
  • Influencer associations

Level 3: Brand Judgments & Brand Feelings ‚Äì What do I think and feel about you?

Judgments (Rational):

  • Is it trustworthy?
  • Is it better than competitors?
  • Is it worth the price?

Feelings (Emotional):

  • Confidence
  • Safety
  • Excitement
  • Convenience
  • Pride

üìå This is where pricing power comes from.

Level 4: Brand Resonance ‚Äì My relationship with you

The pinnacle of brand building:

  • Loyalty and habitual use
  • Emotional attachment
  • Active advocacy (word of mouth)

Digital Signals of Resonance:

  • App stays permanently installed
  • Automatic usage without comparison shopping
  • Defending the brand in online discussions
  • Actively recommending to friends and family

Role of Brand in the Digital Funnel

The digital funnel is faster and more fragile than traditional marketing funnels.

Traditional Funnel

Awareness ‚Üí Consideration ‚Üí Purchase ‚Üí Loyalty

(Slower, media-driven)

Digital Funnel

Search ‚Üí App ‚Üí Experience ‚Üí Review ‚Üí Share

(Faster, experience-driven)

üìå Brand reduces friction at every stage:
  • Strong brand = less comparison
  • Trusted brand = faster decisions
  • Loved brand = repeat usage

Class Activity: The Brand Audit

Objective

To help students identify brand associations, not just features.

Step 1: Brand Pair Assignment

Examples:

  • Uber vs. Didi
  • Fawry vs. InstaPay

Each group audits both brands.

Step 2: Brand Association Mapping

Students map associations under 4 headings:

1. Functional Associations

  • Speed
  • Reliability
  • Ease of use

2. Emotional Associations

  • Trust
  • Stress-free experience
  • Confidence
  • Safety

3. Personality

  • Modern / Traditional
  • Friendly / Corporate
  • Innovative / Bureaucratic

4. Social Meaning

  • Who uses it?
  • Is it "cool," "safe," or "official"?
  • Peer influence factors

Step 3: Discussion Questions

  • Why would a customer pay more for Brand A?
  • Which brand feels safer with money or personal data?
  • Which brand feels more human?
  • How does the app experience reinforce these feelings?
  • What digital moment could destroy trust instantly?

Key Takeaways

  • Products compete on features. Brands compete on meaning.
  • Brand Equity lives in customer memory and emotion.
  • Digital touchpoints don't just support the brand ‚Äî they ARE the brand.
  • Strong brands reduce price sensitivity and increase loyalty.
  • In digital markets, brand equity is built or destroyed faster than ever.

Optional Reflection Question (Homework or Discussion)

"Think of one app you use without comparing alternatives. What brand associations keep you loyal?"
