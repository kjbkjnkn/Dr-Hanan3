Digital Word of Mouth (eWOM)

Discussion Starter

“Before buying anything online, what is the first thing you usually check?”

In the digital world, people don’t just buy products—they buy other people’s experiences. Consumers trust other consumers more than brands.

Traditional vs. Digital WOM

Feature Traditional WOM Digital WOM (eWOM)
Reach Limited (Face-to-face) Global (Platform-based)
Longevity Short-lived Permanent & Searchable
Measurement Hard to track Measurable & Trackable
Audience Personal Network Public / Wide Audience

Types of Digital WOM

Organic eWOM

Happens naturally based on real experiences. (Most trusted type).

Amplified eWOM

Encouraged by brands via referral programs or hashtag campaigns.

Influencer eWOM

Opinions shared by opinion leaders. Trust depends on authenticity.

Negative eWOM

Complaints and viral criticism that can outweigh positive messages.

Impact on Consumer Behavior

eWOM acts as powerful Social Proof, reducing the perceived risk of a purchase.

  • Risk Reduction: Helps consumers feel safer about their choice.
  • Decision Speed: Positive reviews act as a mental shortcut to purchase.
  • Transparency: Consumers look for honest feedback over brand-curated ads.

Brand Management Strategy

Handling Negative eWOM:

  • Empathy First: Acknowledge the problem without being defensive.
  • Quick Response: Show you are listening in real-time.
  • Resolution: Offer a solution publicly to build wider trust.

Key Takeaways

  • Credibility: eWOM is the most trusted source of brand information.
  • Visibility: Digital platforms make consumer voices permanent and searchable.
  • Algorithm Power: Ratings and reviews directly impact search visibility.
  • Active Management: Brands must encourage advocacy and handle complaints professionally.
