Session 05 • Setting Strategic Goals

Communication Objectives

Discussion Starter

“If a campaign gets millions of views but no one remembers the brand, was it successful?”

Communication objectives define what the brand wants to change in the consumer’s mind. While sales are the result, communication focuses on awareness, perception, and attitude.

Marketing vs. Communication

Marketing Objectives

Focus on business outcomes:

  • Increase sales / Revenue
  • Grow market share
  • Enter new territories

Communication Objectives

Focus on consumer mindset:

  • Increase brand awareness
  • Improve brand trust
  • Change negative perceptions
Strategic Insight: Communication objectives support marketing objectives. If you don't know what you want to change in the mind, your message will fail.

Types of Communication Objectives

Following the Hierarchy of Effects Model:

Type The Core Question Example Objective
Awareness Do they know us? Increase brand recall by 30%
Knowledge Do they understand us? Explain a new app feature to 1M users
Attitude How do they feel about us? Position brand as "Safety-First"
Action Will they act? Drive 50,000 free trial sign-ups
Loyalty Will they stay? Increase repeat purchase rate by 15%

SMART Framework

Effective objectives must be Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound.

Bad Objective: "Get more people to like our Facebook page."
SMART Objective: "Increase engagement rate on Instagram by 10% among users aged 18-25 over the next 3 months."

Key Takeaways

  • Mindset over Sales: Objectives define what should change in the consumer's brain.
  • Journey Mapping: Objectives must match the consumer's current stage (Awareness vs. Action).
  • Precision: Digital channels allow us to measure specific behaviors (bounce rate, completion rate).
  • Clarity: Clear objectives prevent wasted budget and inconsistent messaging.
