Module 05 • Verbal Strategy

Brand Voice & Content Strategy

Session Focus

Defining and managing verbal communication across digital platforms to ensure emotional resonance and strategic alignment with brand positioning.

1. Voice vs. Tone

While Voice is the permanent personality of the brand, Tone is the emotional inflection adapted for context.

The Persona Question: "If the brand were a person, how would it speak?"

Customer Support

Tone: Calm, reassuring, and solution-oriented.

Product Launch

Tone: Energetic, inspiring, and bold.

Policy Updates

Tone: Clear, transparent, and direct.

2. Brand Archetypes

Archetypes provide a psychological framework for language choice and humor levels.

The Jester

Style: Casual, humorous, irreverent.

Fit: TikTok, X, Reels.

The Sage

Style: Wise, educational, thoughtful.

Fit: LinkedIn, Whitepapers.

The Hero

Style: Bold, confident, motivational.

Fit: Launch Videos, Manifestos.

3. Content Strategy & Planning

A content strategy connects brand identity with published output through Editorial Calendars.

Micro vs. Macro Content:

Micro-content (TikTok/Tweets) prioritizes Attention. Macro-content (Blogs/Whitepapers) prioritizes Trust and Depth.

Activity: The Translator (15 mins)

Brief: Translate the corporate statement "We are updating our privacy policy" into three archetypes:

Jester: "Cleaning up our policy so it's way less boring."
Sage: "Ensuring greater transparency in data handling."
Hero: "Reinforcing our commitment to your trust."

Key Takeaways

  • Brand voice is a long-term strategic asset.
  • Tone adapts to platform emotion; voice remains consistent.
  • Editorial calendars ensure strategic use of content pillars.
