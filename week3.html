Session 03 • Psychological Insights

Consumer Behaviour

Discussion Starter

Why do two people see the same Instagram ad, but only one clicks "Buy Now"?

Marketing isn't just about what brands say; it's about how consumers think, feel, and act. Understanding this is the key to reducing marketing risk.

Key Idea: Brands don’t sell products. They influence decisions.

What is Consumer Behaviour?

The study of how individuals or groups select, purchase, use, and dispose of products or experiences to satisfy needs and wants.

Thoughts

Perceptions, beliefs, and memory retrieval.

Feelings

Emotions, attitudes, and "gut feelings" toward a brand.

Actions

The actual purchase, usage, and word-of-mouth sharing.

The Decision-Making Process

A mental journey every consumer takes before and after a purchase.

1
Need Recognition: Feeling a gap between current and desired state. (Triggered by hunger, boredom, or an ad).
2
Information Search: Seeking value through internal memory or external sources like Google and YouTube.
3
Evaluation of Alternatives: Comparing options based on price, reviews, and emotional fit.
4
Purchase Decision: Selecting the brand. Note: Friction during checkout can kill this stage!
5
Post-Purchase Behavior: Evaluating satisfaction or feeling cognitive dissonance (regret).

Four Forces of Influence

Decisions are shaped by internal and external drivers:

Psychological

Motivation, Perception, and Learning. One sees "convenient," another sees "expensive."

Personal

Age, income, lifestyle, and personality. A parent and a student shop differently.

Social

Family, TikTok influencers, and "People like you" social proof.

Cultural

Deepest influence. Values, traditions, and social class norms.

The Digital Shift

Traditional Journey

Need → Store → Buy → Feedback. (Slow, limited info).

Digital Journey

Need → Search → Review → Buy → Share. (Fast, transparent, social).

Insight: Consumers think logically but decide emotionally, then justify rationally.

Key Takeaways

  • Marketing success depends on understanding the consumer, not just the product.
  • Digital platforms have shifted power from brands to users.
  • Strong brands reduce the effort required in the "Information Search" stage.
  • Emotions (Trust, Fear, Excitement) drive the majority of final choices.
